Fourth Conjugation Verbs

Verbs from the fourth conjugation end ‘–ire’.

Latin English
audire to hear
scire to know
venire to come

To conjugate these verbs:

1. Remove the ‘-ire’

2. Add these endings:

Latin English
-io I
-is you (singular)
-it he/she/it
-imus we
-itis you (plural)
-iunt they

Look at our example of venire (to come):

Latin English
venio I concede
venis you concede (singular)
venit he/she/it concedes
venimus we concede
venitis you concede (plural)
veniunt they concede

Did you notice any similarities between the endings of regular third and fourth conjugation verbs? Look back at the lesson and check.

The endings are all the same, with the only differences being that in the fourth conjugation, the ‘I’ and ‘they’ forms have an ‘i’.

Noticing patterns like this makes learning Latin much easier.

Here are some other examples:

Latin English

agricole ad curiam veniunt

ad curiam, -e (f.) to the court

 the farmers come to the court
non scio I do not know
